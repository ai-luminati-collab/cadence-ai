# Skill Framework: Marketing Psychology Triggers
> Type: Thinking Methodology (Apply to every content piece)
> When to use: Content generation, copywriting, hook writing, CTA design
> Confidence: High

---

## Mandatory Trigger Selection

For EVERY content piece generated, explicitly select 1-2 primary psychological triggers and embed them structurally. Do not just name the trigger — show how it manifests in the copy/visual.

### The 8 Master Triggers

**1. FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**
- Mechanism: Loss aversion is 2x stronger than gain seeking
- Apply when: Limited offers, trending topics, exclusive access
- Language patterns: "While you're still...", "Everyone in [industry] already...", "The window is closing on..."
- Visual cues: Countdown timers, "X spots left", "Last chance"

**2. STATUS / IDENTITY**
- Mechanism: People buy what signals who they are to their tribe
- Apply when: Premium/aspirational brands, B2B thought leadership, lifestyle brands
- Language patterns: "The kind of [role] who...", "Not for everyone", "If you know, you know"
- Visual cues: Aspirational lifestyle, exclusive aesthetics, in-group signaling

**3. BELONGING / TRIBE**
- Mechanism: Humans are wired to seek group membership and shared identity
- Apply when: Community building, brand movements, user-generated content
- Language patterns: "Join X thousand...", "You're one of us", "The [brand] family"
- Visual cues: Community shots, user photos, group rituals

**4. CURIOSITY GAP**
- Mechanism: An open loop the brain must close — the most powerful hook trigger
- Apply when: Educational content, story-based posts, thread openers
- Language patterns: "The thing nobody tells you about...", "Here's what happened when...", "I was wrong about X"
- RULE: Always close the loop. Curiosity without payoff = unfollows

**5. SOCIAL PROOF**
- Mechanism: We use others' behavior as a shortcut for what's correct
- Apply when: Testimonials, case studies, user counts, media mentions
- Language patterns: "X customers switched", "Featured in [outlet]", "Rated #1 by..."
- Types ranked: Expert proof > Celebrity proof > User proof > Crowd proof > Certification

**6. RECIPROCITY**
- Mechanism: Give value first, people feel compelled to return the favor
- Apply when: Free tools, educational content, templates, audits
- Language patterns: "Free [valuable thing]", "No strings attached", "Here's our entire playbook"
- RULE: The free thing must be genuinely useful, not a teaser that frustrates

**7. AUTHORITY**
- Mechanism: People defer to experts and credentialed sources
- Apply when: Founder content, industry data, expert opinions, certifications
- Language patterns: "After X years in...", "The data shows...", "According to [credible source]"
- Visual cues: Professional settings, data visualizations, logos of known entities

**8. SCARCITY / URGENCY**
- Mechanism: Limited availability increases perceived value
- Apply when: Launches, promotions, seasonal content, limited editions
- Language patterns: "Only X remaining", "Ends [specific date]", "Once it's gone..."
- RULE: Must be REAL scarcity. Fake urgency destroys trust permanently

---

## Application Protocol

When generating any content piece:
1. Identify which trigger(s) match the post's goal and audience awareness state
2. Embed the trigger in the HOOK (first 3 seconds)
3. Reinforce the trigger in the CTA
4. Note the trigger in the content metadata so the brand team knows the strategic intent
5. Never stack more than 2 triggers — it feels manipulative and reduces trust
