# The Book Stack -- Key Principles and Frameworks
> Topic: Canonical marketing books distilled into actionable frameworks
> Layer: Reference
> Freshness: Evergreen
> Confidence: High

---

## Marketing Management (Kotler/Keller/Chernev)
The STP framework, 4Ps (Product, Price, Place, Promotion), marketing planning process, brand equity models, customer value proposition. The strategic backbone of modern marketing: all tactical decisions flow from STP.

## Positioning (Ries & Trout)
First-mover advantage in the mind. The ladder of brands (consumers hold mental rankings). Category creation. Being first in a category matters more than being better. If you can't be first, create a new category. The mind rejects what doesn't fit existing mental models.

## How Brands Grow (Byron Sharp)
Mental availability and physical availability are the two growth levers. Double jeopardy law (small brands have fewer customers who are also less loyal). Most brand buyers are light buyers. Loyalty is a function of market share, not a lever to pull. Mass marketing to all category buyers outperforms narrow targeting.

## Obviously Awesome (April Dunford)
Five components of positioning: competitive alternatives, unique attributes, value those attributes enable, target customer characteristics, market category. Positioning is not messaging. Mispositioned products fail regardless of quality. The "positioning canvas" exercise.

## Scientific Advertising (Claude Hopkins)
Direct response principles. Every ad should be measured. Testing is essential. Headlines matter more than body copy. Specificity sells (numbers, facts, details beat vague claims). The scientific method applied to advertising.

## Influence (Robert Cialdini)
Six principles of influence: Reciprocity, Commitment/Consistency, Social Proof, Authority, Liking, Scarcity. These are deeply embedded human psychological patterns. Apply ethically across all marketing touchpoints.

## This Is Marketing (Seth Godin)
Smallest viable audience. Marketing is about change, not manipulation. Permission over interruption. Status roles (affiliation vs. dominance) drive behavior. Enrollment: people choose to be on the journey.

## Permission Marketing (Seth Godin)
Anticipated, personal, relevant messaging. Build a permission asset. Interrupt-based marketing has declining returns. The value exchange: give something valuable to earn attention. Your email list and subscribers are your most valuable marketing assets.

## Building a StoryBrand 2.0 (Donald Miller)
SB7 Framework: Character (customer) has a Problem (external, internal, philosophical), meets a Guide (brand with empathy and authority), who gives a Plan, calls to Action, helps Avoid Failure, and ends in Success. Clarity beats cleverness. Customers buy what they understand fastest.

## Contagious (Jonah Berger)
STEPPS framework: Social Currency (sharing makes people look good), Triggers (environmental reminders), Emotion (high-arousal emotions drive sharing), Public (observable = talkable), Practical Value (useful = shared), Stories (narratives carry information further).

## Hacking Growth (Sean Ellis & Morgan Brown)
Growth teams as cross-functional units. North Star Metric as the single number that best captures value delivery. Activation optimization (the aha moment). Retention loops. Experimentation velocity: the more experiments you run, the faster you grow. The growth process: analyze, ideate, prioritize, test.

## Play Bigger (Ramadan, Peterson, Lochhead, Maney)
Category design: don't compete in existing categories; create new ones. Lightning Strikes (orchestrated launches that define the category). Category kings capture 76% of market value. The Frozen Middle (companies stuck between niche and category king).
