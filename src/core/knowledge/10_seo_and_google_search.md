## How Google Search Works (2026)

Google Search operates through three stages: Crawling (discovering content via links and sitemaps), Indexing (analyzing and storing content), and Serving/Ranking (determining the best results for a query).

---

## Core Ranking Systems (2026)

Helpful Content System: Fully integrated into core updates as of 2024. No longer standalone. Reduced unhelpful/low-quality content by 45%. Sites optimized for search engines instead of users saw heavy ranking losses. AI-generated or unoriginal content was hit hardest.

E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness): Not a direct ranking factor but a framework Google uses to evaluate content quality. Experience (first-hand experience with the topic) was added as a signal. Author credentials, site reputation, and transparent sourcing all matter.

RankBrain, BERT, MUM: Google's AI systems for understanding search intent. RankBrain handles novel queries, BERT understands natural language context, MUM operates across languages and modalities. Google uses deeper AI models in 2026 to classify search intent. If content doesn't align with intent, it won't rank even if well optimized.

Mobile-first indexing: Google indexes the mobile version of your site first. 63% of global web traffic is mobile. Poor mobile UX equals poor rankings.

## Core Web Vitals (2026)

Google's three user experience metrics functioning as ranking signals:
- LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): Loading performance. Target: under 2.5 seconds.
- INP (Interaction to Next Paint): Replaced FID in 2024. Measures full responsiveness. Target: under 200 milliseconds.
- CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): Visual stability. Target: under 0.1.

New in 2026: VSI (Visual Stability Index) measures layout stability throughout the entire user session, not just initial load.

Only about 54.6% of websites pass all Core Web Vitals checks. Sites optimizing to these thresholds see 12-20% organic traffic increases and 15-30% conversion rate improvements. CWV functions as a tiebreaker: when content quality and authority are similar, superior UX wins.

AMP no longer provides ranking advantages in 2026. Well-optimized standard HTML achieves identical or better scores.

## AI Overviews / AI Search (2026)

Google's AI Overviews generate synthesized answers at the top of search results. To appear:
- Structure content for extraction: summaries, question-based headings, clear definitions, logical formatting
- Build topical authority: consistent depth across related topics, not isolated pieces
- Use structured data (Schema markup) for featured snippets and rich results
- Content must be easy for AI to understand, extract, and summarize
- Brand signals across platforms help AI systems trust your content
- AI doesn't rank content; it selects and synthesizes it. If content isn't structured for extraction, it won't be visible.

---

## Structured Data / Schema Markup

Schema helps Google understand content faster and improves chances of appearing in featured snippets and AI Overview highlights. Key types: FAQ, HowTo, Article, Product, Review, LocalBusiness, Organization.

---

## SEO Strategy for 2026

- Prioritize original, experience-backed, people-first content
- Build topic clusters with interlinked content (semantic authority)
- Focus on semantic meaning, not keyword stuffing. Google's NLP understands intent.
- Video content is a major visibility driver (YouTube, Google Discover, video snippets)
- Local SEO signals now influence AI visibility
- Content lifespan: evergreen content can resurface months or years later when topics become relevant again
- Only 54.6% of sites pass CWV; fixing yours is a competitive advantage

---

## Anti-patterns (What NOT to Do)

- Keyword stuffing
- Thin, AI-generated content without human oversight
- Ignoring mobile optimization
- Buying low-quality backlinks (spam detection sharper than ever)
- Publishing duplicate content
- Ignoring structured data
- Prioritizing quantity over quality (one comprehensive piece outperforms ten thin ones)
- Chasing rankings instead of revenue
