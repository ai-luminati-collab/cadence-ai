## How Instagram's Algorithm Works (2026)

Instagram does not use one algorithm. It uses multiple AI-powered ranking systems, each tailored to a different surface: Feed, Reels, Stories, and Explore. Each system evaluates content using its own criteria.

---

## Feed Algorithm

The Feed shows content from accounts you follow plus recommended posts. Ranking factors by importance:

1. Relationship strength: How often the viewer interacts with the poster. DMs, comments, story replies, and profile visits all count.
2. User engagement patterns: What a specific user likes, shares, saves, or comments on.
3. Content performance: How many people engage with a post, how quickly engagement happens, post timing and location.
4. Poster activity: How frequently the account posts and engages with others.

---

## Reels Algorithm

The three most important signals, confirmed by Adam Mosseri in January 2025 and still dominant in 2026:

1. Watch time: The single most important signal across all surfaces. How long viewers stay with the content. A viewer who watches 100% of an 8-minute video sends a stronger signal than one who watches 40% of a 25-minute video.
2. Sends per reach (DM shares): DM shares are 3-5x more valuable than likes for reaching new audiences. Instagram explicitly weights shares heavily because they signal genuine connection.
3. Likes per reach: Still matters but carries less weight than watch time or sends.

Additional Reels signals:
- Originality Score: Instagram uses AI to detect recycled clips. Reposting TikTok videos with watermarks or reusing trending content verbatim significantly reduces reach. Accounts posting 10+ reposts within 30 days get excluded from recommendations entirely.
- Longer Reels (up to 3 minutes): Now get recommended to non-followers. Instagram is actively rewarding longer storytelling, provided content sustains watch time.
- Conversation depth: Instagram no longer counts simple "nice!" comments as meaningful. It tracks long comments, replies between users, and extended threads. Comments longer than five words get classified as "high social relevance."
- Topic clusters: The algorithm tries to understand what your account is about, not just individual posts. Accounts with a cohesive niche get distributed more widely within that community. Multi-niche accounts are penalized.
- Caption dwell time: When users spend time reading a caption, the post is treated as more valuable.
- Audio: Voiceovers and original audio get algorithmic preference. Captions (subtitles) are a confirmed Reels ranking factor and help Instagram's AI categorize content.

---

## Stories Algorithm

Stories use a dedicated AI system that predicts which stories you'll find most valuable. Ranking signals:
- How likely you are to click into the story
- How likely you are to reply or react
- How close your relationship is (based on DMs, interactions)
- Viewing frequency and history

Interactive features (polls, sliders, Q&As, quizzes) boost Story ranking because they drive reply and reaction signals.

---

## Explore Algorithm

Explore surfaces content from accounts you don't follow. It evaluates:
- Your activity in Explore (past likes, saves, comments, shares)
- The post's popularity signals (engagement velocity)
- Your interaction history with similar content and similar creators
- The poster's recent engagement patterns

---

## Major 2026 Changes

- "Your Algorithm" feature (launched December 2025, global early 2026): Users can now review topics Instagram thinks they care about and add/remove categories. Makes niche focus even more critical.
- AI-powered translations for Reels: Instagram can translate text and audio into Hindi, Portuguese, English, and Spanish.
- "Views" as the primary metric: Instagram unified performance measurement across all formats under a single "Views" metric.
- Trial Reels: Let creators test content with non-followers before pushing to their audience.
- Cross-Meta engagement signals: Instagram now factors in engagement from across Meta's platforms.

---

## Recommendation Eligibility (What Gets You Excluded)

Content that violates Recommendation Guidelines won't appear in Explore or Reels recommendations even if it's allowed on the platform. Examples: recycled/reposted content, watermarked videos, low-resolution imagery, content with excessive text overlays, misleading hooks.

---

## Instagram Optimization Rules

- Optimize for shares (DM sends), not just likes. Create content people want to send to a friend.
- Post Reels consistently (3-5/week minimum). Mix polished with candid.
- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags maximum. Keyword-stuffed captions no longer help if the actual visual/audio content doesn't match.
- Add captions/subtitles to all video content.
- Maintain a cohesive niche. Topic consistency > post frequency.
- Use carousel posts fully (10 slides). Instagram reshows carousels with unseen slides, creating re-engagement.
- Respond to comments to trigger "high social relevance" signals.
- Post Stories daily with interactive elements.
- Likes are now the weakest engagement signal. Hierarchy: Watch time > Shares/DM sends > Saves > Comments (long) > Likes.
- 3-5 Reels per week and daily Stories is recommended for consistent growth.
- Reels with strong 3-second hold rates (above 60%) outperform those below 40% by 5-10x in total reach.
- Engagement benchmarks: Under 5K followers average 3.79% engagement rate. Smaller accounts have a significant advantage for organic growth.
