## B2B REVENUE MARKETING

### Demand Generation vs. Lead Generation

Demand gen creates awareness and interest before someone is ready to buy. It educates the market and builds category awareness. Lead gen captures information from people already interested. The mistake: treating all leads as equally valuable. The fix: demand gen first, lead capture when intent signals are present.

### Account-Based Marketing (ABM)

ABM inverts the traditional funnel. Identify target accounts first, then create personalized campaigns.

Tiers:
- 1:1 ABM (Strategic): Custom campaigns for individual high-value accounts
- 1:Few ABM (Lite): Personalized campaigns for clusters of similar accounts
- 1:Many ABM (Programmatic): Targeted advertising to a broader account list using technology

LinkedIn is the primary ABM channel. Works best when sales and marketing are aligned on account lists, ICP definitions, and success metrics.

### Content Syndication

Distributing gated content through third-party publishers to generate ICP-matched leads. Quality depends on partner's audience matching and lead validation. Always verify leads against ICP criteria post-delivery.

### Lead Scoring

Assign points based on:
- Demographic fit (title, company size, industry) = "fit score"
- Behavioral signals (page visits, downloads, email engagement, webinar attendance) = "engagement score"
- Negative scoring for non-ICP characteristics
- Define MQL and SQL thresholds with sales alignment

LinkedIn's 2025 B2B benchmark: trust and social validation matter more than product attributes or price in brand influence. Score leads for value, not just volume.

### Brand-to-Demand

Connecting brand marketing to demand gen outcomes. Pipeline: brand awareness builds trust, trust lowers CAC, lower CAC enables profitable scaling.

## D2C / LIFECYCLE COMMERCE

### Key D2C Principles

- Customer experience control is the primary advantage
- First-party data capture is critical (email, SMS, account creation)
- Retention economics: Acquiring new customers costs 5-7x more than retaining existing
- First 30 days after first purchase determine repeat purchase behavior

### Merchandising Strategy

- Hero product: One flagship SKU that drives awareness and trial
- Product bundles: Increase AOV and reduce decision fatigue
- Subscription models: Predictable revenue, higher LTV (requires genuine value)
- Limited editions/drops: Create urgency and brand heat

### PDP (Product Detail Page) Optimization

- 5-6+ high-quality images from multiple angles
- Video demonstrations
- Benefit-driven copy (not just specs)
- Social proof (reviews, ratings, UGC photos)
- Shipping, return, sizing info above the fold
- Cross-sell recommendations
- Mobile-first design

### Retention Systems

- Post-purchase email flows (education, cross-sell, review request)
- Loyalty programs (points, tiers, VIP access)
- Referral programs with double-sided incentives
- Community building (private groups, social engagement)
- Replenishment reminders (consumable products)
- Surprise and delight (unexpected gifts, handwritten notes, birthday offers)

### Returns as Marketing

Returns are a marketing channel, not just a cost center. Easy returns build trust and increase future purchases. Track return rate by acquisition channel and product to identify quality/expectation gaps.

### Unified Commerce

Shopify frames D2C success around control of customer experience, retention systems, and recurring revenue mechanics. The modern D2C stack integrates merchandising, marketing, fulfillment, customer service, and data into a single system.
