> Layer: Advanced
> Freshness: Q1 2026

---

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Development

For B2B: Define by company attributes (industry, size, revenue, tech stack, funding stage) AND buyer attributes (title, seniority, department, pain points). Best ICPs include both firmographic and psychographic data.

For D2C: Define by demographic + psychographic + behavioral signals. What do they buy, why, how often, and what triggers a purchase?

---

## Personas vs. ICPs

ICPs describe the ideal company or buyer profile at a strategic level. Personas are fictional narrative representations of specific user types within the ICP. ICPs guide targeting and qualification. Personas guide messaging, creative, and content strategy.

---

## Buying Groups (B2B)

Enterprise purchases involve 6-10 decision-makers: economic buyer, technical buyer, user buyer, champion, gatekeeper. Content and ad targeting must address the full buying group, not just one persona. Forrester points to larger, more digital, more self-serve buying behavior in B2B.

---

## Behavioral Cohorts

Segment by behavior, not just demographics:
- Purchase frequency (one-time, repeat, loyal)
- Engagement recency (active, lapsed, dormant)
- Content affinity clusters (topics they engage with)
- Channel preference (email responders, social engagers, search-first)

---

## Personalization Principles

- Email: Dynamic content based on purchase history, browse behavior, lifecycle stage
- Paid social: Creative variations by audience segment
- Web: Dynamic landing pages, personalized recommendations, geo-targeted content
- Key principle: Personalization should feel helpful, not creepy.

## Creator Discovery

Sources: TikTok Creator Marketplace, Instagram Creator Marketplace, YouTube BrandConnect, manual search within niche hashtags, agency partnerships.

Evaluation criteria: audience demographics (not just size), engagement rate (3-8% strong for micro), content quality and style alignment, audience overlap with ICP, past brand collaboration performance, authenticity of following.

---

## Influencer Tiers

- Nano (1K-10K): Highest engagement, most authentic, lowest cost. Best for local/niche.
- Micro (10K-100K): Strong engagement, niche authority, affordable. Best ROI for most brands.
- Mid-tier (100K-500K): Broader reach, still relatable. Good for launches.
- Macro (500K-1M): Significant reach, lower engagement. Awareness.
- Mega (1M+): Massive reach, lowest engagement, highest cost. Celebrity play.

Micro-influencers consistently deliver best ROI. TikTok data: micro (10K-50K) see 8%+ engagement, outperforming macro-influencers.

---

## Partnership Ads / Whitelisting

Running paid ads through a creator's handle rather than the brand's page. Benefits: native feel, creator trust, access to creator's first-party data. One of the highest-performing ad formats across Meta and TikTok.

---

## UGC Asset Pipelines

1. Recruit creators (paid UGC creators create for the brand to use, not their own audience)
2. Clear briefs: hook, key message, CTA, do's/don'ts
3. Batch production: 5-10 videos per creator per sprint
4. A/B test UGC vs. polished creative in paid campaigns
5. Content library organized by angle, hook, and format
