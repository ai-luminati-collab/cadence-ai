## Headline and Hook Frameworks

The four headline functions (from classic copywriting): Useful, Urgent, Ultra-specific, Unique. Headlines should accomplish at least one, ideally two or more.

Hook structures for short-form content:
- Pattern Interrupt: Start with something unexpected that breaks the scroll
- Curiosity Gap: Tease an outcome without revealing the answer
- Bold Claim: Make a specific, provocative statement that demands engagement
- Relatable Problem: Open with a pain point the audience recognizes instantly
- Social Proof Lead: Start with a result or testimonial

For paid ads specifically, the hook must appear in the first 3 seconds of video or the first line of text. 50% of viewers drop off in the first 3 seconds of a Reel or TikTok. The hook is the ad.

## CTA Design Principles

Strong CTAs are specific (not "Learn More" but "Get the 2026 Playbook"), action-oriented (start with a verb), low-friction (match the CTA to the commitment level the audience is ready for), and visually distinct (contrast, whitespace, directional cues). CTA copy should align with funnel stage: top-funnel gets "Watch," "Explore," "Discover"; mid-funnel gets "Download," "Register," "Compare"; bottom-funnel gets "Buy Now," "Start Free Trial," "Book a Demo."

## Landing Page Copy Architecture

The conversion-optimized landing page follows this hierarchy:
1. Headline: Clearly state the primary benefit or outcome
2. Sub-headline: Add specificity or address the target audience
3. Hero section: Visual proof (product in use, UI screenshot, video)
4. Problem agitation: Describe the pain state the audience is in
5. Solution reveal: Position the product as the bridge to the desired state
6. Features as benefits: Each feature mapped to a real outcome
7. Social proof: Testimonials, logos, stats, case studies
8. Objection handling: FAQ or comparison section addressing top hesitations
9. CTA repetition: Minimum 2-3 CTAs on any landing page
10. Urgency/scarcity: If authentic and ethical

## Ad Angles and Messaging Frameworks

An "angle" is the strategic lens through which you present a product. Common angles include:
- Problem/Solution: Lead with the pain, present the product as the fix
- Before/After: Show the transformation
- Us vs. Them: Compare to alternatives (direct or implied)
- Social Proof: Lead with results others have achieved
- Story-driven: Lead with a narrative that the audience relates to
- Education-first: Teach something valuable, then position the product
- Contrarian: Challenge an assumption the audience holds

## Building a StoryBrand (Donald Miller) -- SB7 Framework

The Customer is the Hero. The Brand is the Guide. The framework:
1. A Character (the customer) has a Problem (external, internal, philosophical)
2. And meets a Guide (the brand, who has empathy and authority)
3. Who gives them a Plan (simple, clear steps)
4. And calls them to Action (direct CTA and transitional CTA)
5. That helps them Avoid Failure (stakes/negative consequences)
6. And ends in Success (the positive transformation)

The core insight: Customers don't buy the best products. They buy the products they can understand the fastest. Clarity beats cleverness.

## UGC and Creator-Style Content

User-generated content now outperforms polished brand creative across Meta, TikTok, and increasingly YouTube and LinkedIn. The reasons: platform algorithms reward authenticity signals, users scroll past content that looks like ads, and UGC carries implicit social proof.

UGC production categories: unboxing/first impression, problem-solution testimonial, day-in-the-life integration, comparison/review, how-to/tutorial. The most effective UGC follows a hook-story-CTA structure and maintains a single clear message per video.

## Visual Hierarchy for Digital

The F-pattern and Z-pattern describe how users scan web content. Key rules: the eye enters top-left, larger elements draw attention first, contrast creates hierarchy, whitespace creates emphasis, and directional cues (arrows, gaze direction in photos) guide the eye toward CTAs.

For social media creatives: bold, readable text at the top; high-contrast imagery; product visible within the first frame; brand logo small and non-intrusive; aspect ratios native to the platform (9:16 for Stories/Reels/TikTok, 4:5 for feed, 1:1 for carousel).
