# Agentic Marketing Orchestration
> Topic: Prompt frameworks, brand voice constraints, hallucination checks, human approval gates
> Layer: Super-Advanced (Elite)
> Freshness: Q1 2026
> Confidence: High (product design principles)

---

## Prompt Frameworks by Task

The AI agent should use structured prompts for different marketing tasks:

Campaign brief generation: Input (product, audience, objective, budget, channels, timeline) produces structured brief with messaging, creative direction, targeting, KPIs.

Creative ideation: Input (brand voice, format constraints, angle type) produces 5-10 creative concepts with rationale.

Performance analysis: Input (metrics data, benchmarks, previous period) produces what happened, why, what to do next.

Audience research: Input (product category, competitive landscape) produces ICP definition, persona narratives, JTBD analysis.

---

## Brand Voice Constraints

The AI agent must maintain brand voice consistency. Define voice along: tone (formal/casual), personality (authoritative/friendly/irreverent), vocabulary (technical/accessible), values (what the brand stands for and against).

---

## Hallucination Checks

- Check knowledge base first for factual claims
- Flag unverified information
- Never invent statistics, case study results, or platform rules
- Always cite source and freshness date

---

## Human Approval Gates

Flag for human review:
- Any content going live publicly
- Budget recommendations above defined thresholds
- Creative referencing competitors, legal claims, or sensitive topics
- Strategy recommendations contradicting brand guidelines

---

## Prompt-Ready Output Formats

Campaign Brief:
Brand, Objective, Target Audience, Channels (with rationale), Budget (total and per-channel), Timeline, Key Message, Creative Direction, KPIs, Measurement Plan.

Creative Brief:
Format, Platform, Objective, Audience, Hook (first 3 seconds/first line), Key Message, CTA, Tone, References, Specs.

Performance Report:
Period, What Happened (vs. targets), Why It Happened (root cause), What to Do Next (prioritized recommendations), Risks.
