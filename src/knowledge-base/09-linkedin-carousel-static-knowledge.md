# LINKEDIN CAROUSEL (Document Post)

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Cover Slide**: First page of the PDF carousel. The hook. Professional but bold. Clear topic signal that makes someone want to swipe. Examples: "The 7 frameworks every marketer should steal" or "What nobody tells you about scaling a B2B team."

2. **Slides (Array of content pages)**: One insight per page. 8-12 pages is optimal. Each page has a heading plus 2-3 bullet points or a short paragraph. Content should be scannable and each page should deliver standalone value.

3. **Closing Slide**: Final page. Recap the single biggest takeaway. Clear CTA (follow, comment, save). Include the author's name and handle for attribution when the carousel is shared or screenshotted.

4. **Post Caption**: Written caption accompanying the document upload. 50-150 words. Provides context on why you created this. Structure: hook in first 2 lines, brief summary of what the reader will learn, CTA to read/swipe.

5. **Hashtags**: 3-5 industry-specific hashtags in the caption.

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES FOR LINKEDIN CAROUSELS

### Why Document Posts Win on LinkedIn
- Native documents (PDF carousels) are the #1 performing format on LinkedIn in 2026 with a 7% average engagement rate, up 14% year-over-year
- Each swipe through a page signals interest to the algorithm and increases dwell time, which is a primary ranking factor
- Multi-slide PDFs generate 3x more engagement than single images
- The format is excellent for frameworks, processes, checklists, and step-by-step guides that professionals save for reference
- Pages should be designed at 1080x1350px or 1080x1080px for optimal display in the LinkedIn feed

### Content That Works in LinkedIn Carousels
- Industry frameworks and mental models
- Step-by-step processes with real-world examples
- Data visualizations, benchmark reports, and research summaries
- Career advice and professional development guides
- Listicles with depth: "10 Mistakes in [Topic]" with one mistake per slide and specific explanation
- Comparison posts: "Tool A vs Tool B" or "Strategy 1 vs Strategy 2" with structured analysis
- Checklists that professionals can screenshot and use immediately

### Design Expectations
- LinkedIn audience expects more professional polish than Instagram
- Clean typography, structured layouts, readable at a glance
- Brand consistency across all slides (colors, fonts, layout grid)
- Include page numbers or progress indicators so readers know how far they are

# LINKEDIN STATIC (Image Post)

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Image Headline**: Primary text on the image. Professional tone. A bold statement, key data point, or provocative question. Maximum 10 words.

2. **Image Body**: Supporting text on image. Brief expansion or context. Maximum 20 words.

3. **Caption**: Post caption. 100-250 words. Professional narrative format. The image is the visual hook that stops the scroll; the caption delivers the substance, context, and professional insight.

4. **Hashtags**: 3-5 industry-specific.

5. **Visual Directive**: Design guidance. Professional color palette. Data-driven or infographic-style design where relevant. Clean, not cluttered.

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES FOR LINKEDIN STATICS

### When to Use LinkedIn Statics
- Data visualization or single statistic callout that tells a story
- Quote graphics featuring the poster's own quotes or insights (not generic inspirational quotes from famous people)
- Infographics summarizing research, frameworks, or processes
- Announcement graphics (hiring, events, milestones, awards)
- Multi-image albums (upload 3-4 related images together): these are the clear winner for generating likes across all follower counts

### What to Avoid
- Generic stock imagery that looks like every other LinkedIn post
- Overdesigned graphics that look like advertisements rather than professional content
- Motivational quotes from famous people without original insight added
- Low-resolution or poorly cropped images that look unprofessional
