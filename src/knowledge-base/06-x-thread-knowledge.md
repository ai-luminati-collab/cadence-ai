## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Hook Tweet**: First tweet in the thread. Must be compelling as a standalone post AND as an entry point to the thread. Include thread indicator: "🧵" emoji or "(thread)" or "A thread on..." Maximum 280 characters.

2. **Thread Body**: Array of numbered tweets (5-12 tweets optimal). Each tweet contains one point or one idea. Each tweet must be independently retweetable on its own, meaning it should deliver value even without the context of the surrounding tweets.

3. **Closing Tweet**: Final tweet. Summarizes the key takeaway in one line. Includes a CTA: "If you found this useful, RT the first tweet" or "Follow @handle for more on [topic]." Can include a link here (only in the final tweet, never in the hook).

4. **Hashtags**: For the hook tweet only. 1-2 maximum.

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES

### Thread Architecture
- The hook tweet determines whether anyone reads the rest. It must work as a standalone viral tweet AND as a compelling entry point that makes people want to read more.
- Each individual tweet in the thread should be independently valuable. People retweet individual tweets from threads, so every tweet needs to stand on its own.
- Number your tweets for clarity: "1/", "2/", "3/" etc. This helps readers track progress and signals the thread's length.
- 7-12 tweets is the sweet spot for length. Under 5 feels thin and like it could have been a single tweet. Over 15 loses readers to fatigue.
- End with a CTA that serves the thread's purpose: "If this was useful, RT the first tweet so others can find it" or "Follow me for more threads on [topic]"
- Include a self-reply at the end linking back to the first tweet for easy navigation

### Format Tips for Threads
- Use emojis as bullet markers for scanability within individual tweets
- One concept per tweet, never cram two ideas into one
- Short paragraphs within tweets: 2-3 sentences maximum per tweet
- Include data, statistics, or specific numbers when possible for credibility
- Use a mix of insight tweets and example tweets (theory + application)

### What Makes Great Thread Hooks
- "I spent [time] studying [topic]. Here's what I found:" (research-based authority)
- "Most people get [topic] wrong. Here's why:" (contrarian knowledge)
- "[Result] in [timeframe]. Here's the exact playbook:" (result-driven curiosity)
- "I made [mistake] and it cost me [consequence]. The lesson:" (vulnerability + learning)
- "The [number] rules of [topic] that nobody talks about:" (insider knowledge)
