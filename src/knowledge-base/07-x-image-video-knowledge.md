# X IMAGE AND VIDEO POSTS: Platform-Native Content Knowledge
## Platform: X (Twitter) | Format: Image Post + Video Post | Last Updated: April 2026

---

# X IMAGE / VISUAL POST

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Tweet Copy**: Tweet text accompanying the image. Maximum 280 characters. The text and image should work together as a pair, not repeat each other. The tweet provides context, opinion, or the "so what" while the image provides the visual evidence or hook.

2. **Image Directive**: Visual direction for the attached image. What the image shows, composition, text overlay if any. Optimal dimensions: 1200x675px (16:9 landscape) or 1080x1350px (4:5 portrait for more feed real estate).

3. **Image Text Overlay (if applicable)**: Text on the image itself. Maximum 6-8 words. Bold, high-contrast, single clear message. Less than 20% of total image area should be text.

4. **Alt Text**: Accessibility description for screen readers. 150-250 characters describing the image content.

5. **Hashtags**: 1-2 maximum.

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES FOR IMAGE POSTS

### Why Images Matter on X
- Tweets with images receive approximately 150% more engagement than text-only tweets
- Images that perform best on X: real product photos (over staged stock photography), bold text overlays with a single clear message, data visualizations and charts, and before/after comparisons
- X's algorithm favors visual and rich media content over posts containing external links

### Image Specs
- Single image: 1200x675px (16:9) recommended for full-width timeline display without cropping
- Portrait format: 1080x1350px (4:5) also fully supported and displays taller in feed, capturing more scroll-stopping attention
- Multi-image posts: up to 4 images. Keep all images the same aspect ratio for a consistent grid layout. Mixing aspect ratios leads to unpredictable cropping.
- File formats: JPG for photographs, PNG for graphics and text-heavy images (X's JPG compression introduces visible artifacts on hard geometric shapes and text)
- Keep important elements (faces, text, logos) in the center 60-70% of the image; X crops aggressively on mobile and in replies/embeds

### Copy + Image Relationship
- The tweet text should complement the image, never describe it or repeat it
- For data/stat graphics: the tweet provides the "so what" interpretation of the data
- For product images: the tweet tells the story or context behind the product
- For meme-style images: the tweet adds context, tags the audience, or provides the setup
- For screenshots (results, DMs, reviews): the tweet frames why this matters

### Content Types That Perform Best
- Single bold stat or data point as text-on-image with commentary in the tweet
- Before/after comparisons (product results, transformations, progress)
- Screenshots of real results, dashboards, or proof with "Here's what happened" tweet
- Infographic-style single cards with one key takeaway
- Memes and relatable visual content with a brand-relevant twist
- Customer testimonial screenshots with the brand's response

# X VIDEO POST

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Tweet Copy**: Tweet text accompanying the video. Maximum 280 characters. Functions as the "why should I press play" hook. Must create enough curiosity or promise enough value that someone taps to watch.

2. **Video Script**: Script or concept for the video. Scene-by-scene breakdown with timing. Total maximum length: 2 minutes 20 seconds for organic posts. Sweet spot: 15-45 seconds.

3. **Hook Moment**: What happens in the first 3 seconds. Must stop the scroll. Can be a visual hook (unexpected image, movement) or verbal hook (bold opening statement).

4. **Caption Overlays**: Text that appears on-screen throughout the video. Mandatory because most X users watch video on mute. Maximum 6 words per overlay. Synchronized with key moments.

5. **Hashtags**: 1-2 maximum.

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES FOR VIDEO POSTS

### Video on X
- Video is a major engagement driver on X. Supported formats: MP4 and MOV
- Maximum length: 2 minutes 20 seconds for organic posts
- Captions and subtitles are mandatory for accessibility and engagement; a large portion of users watch with sound off
- Shorter videos (15-45 seconds) consistently perform best for organic content engagement
- The tweet text serves as the "why should I watch this" hook and is critical for driving play rate
- Native video always outperforms external video links (YouTube, Vimeo); X deprioritizes posts with outbound links
- Auto-play in the feed means the first frame and first second of motion determine whether someone stops scrolling
