# META STATIC: Platform-Native Content Knowledge
## Platform: Instagram/Facebook | Format: Static Image Post | Last Updated: April 2026

---

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Creative Headline**: Primary text on the image. Maximum 8 words. One focal message. Must be readable at thumbnail size on mobile.

2. **Creative Body (optional)**: Supporting text on image. Maximum 15 words. Only include if it adds meaningful context. Not every static needs body text; sometimes the headline alone is more powerful.

3. **Caption**: Post caption. 50-200 words. The caption does the heavy lifting for statics because the image is the hook and the caption delivers the substance. Structure: hook line, value or context, CTA. 3-5 hashtags at end.

4. **Hashtags**: 3-5 total.

5. **Visual Directive**: Image direction. Specify: photography vs illustration vs graphic design. Color treatment and mood. Composition and focal point. Product placement if relevant. Whether the image should feel polished/editorial or raw/authentic.

---

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES

### When to Use Statics
- Bold single statements, quotes, or provocative opinions
- Product hero shots with minimal text overlay
- Announcements (launches, milestones, achievements)
- Memes and relatable text-on-image content
- Data visualizations or single statistics that tell a story
- Quote graphics (brand's own quotes, not generic inspirational quotes)

### Design Rules
- If the message needs more than 10 words on the image, it should be a carousel instead
- One focal message per static. If you squint at the thumbnail and cannot get the gist, simplify further
- Minimum 24pt font for headlines on 1080x1080 images
- Use brand colors deliberately: primary color for trust and recognition, accent color for drawing attention to key elements
- Avoid clutter; generous whitespace signals premium quality
- Format options: 1080x1080 (square) or 1080x1350 (portrait, provides more real estate in the feed and captures more attention while scrolling)

### When NOT to Use Statics
- If the content is educational with multiple points (use carousel)
- If the content needs a demonstration or movement (use Reel)
- If the content is time-sensitive and ephemeral (use Story)
- If you are trying to maximize reach to non-followers (use Reel; statics have limited discovery potential compared to Reels)
