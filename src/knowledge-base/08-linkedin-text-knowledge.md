# LINKEDIN TEXT POST: Platform-Native Content Knowledge
## Platform: LinkedIn | Format: Text Post | Last Updated: April 2026

---

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Hook (First 2 lines)**: The most important part of the entire post. These are the only lines visible before the "...see more" truncation on mobile. Maximum 20 words. Must be personal, specific, or provocative enough to compel the click.

2. **Body Narrative**: Main post body. Storytelling format. 150-300 words. Short paragraphs of 1-2 sentences each. Line break between every paragraph for readability. Written like a conversation with a professional peer, not a press release.

3. **Closing CTA**: Final lines of the post. Either a specific question that invites genuine comment OR a bold concluding statement that leaves an impression. Not engagement bait like "Agree?" or "Comment YES if..." but a real question: "What's the one change that made the biggest difference in your team's output?"

4. **Hashtags**: 3-5 maximum. Industry-specific and relevant. Placed at the very end, separated from the body by a line break.

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES

### LinkedIn Algorithm 2026
- LinkedIn evaluates "professional relevance scoring": saves, thoughtful comments, and shares matter most for distribution
- The algorithm tests posts with a small group from your network first. If engagement is strong (especially quality comments), it expands reach to broader audiences. If engagement is weak in the first hour, the post dies.
- Experience-based, raw storytelling consistently outperforms polished brand announcements
- LinkedIn now scans post copy for contextual keywords rather than relying on hashtags for topic classification. Writing naturally with relevant industry terms matters more than appending hashtag lists.
- Posts that clearly signal intent and value perform better than generic posts
- LinkedIn actively downranks: overly promotional content, engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree"), generic motivational quotes, and artificial engagement from pods

### The "See More" Click Is Everything
- LinkedIn truncates posts after approximately 2 lines on mobile (about 20-25 words)
- The first 2 lines must create enough curiosity or emotional resonance that the reader taps "...see more"
- Hook formats that consistently work: personal failure + lesson ("I got fired 3 years ago. Best thing that happened to me."), counter-intuitive insight ("Stop networking. Do this instead."), specific result with promise ("How we reduced churn by 34% with one email."), vulnerable admission ("I almost gave up on this company last month.")
- Hook formats that consistently fail: "I'm thrilled to announce...", "Excited to share...", "As a [job title], I believe...", "Happy to report...", generic inspirational quotes

### Body Writing Style
- Short paragraphs are essential. One sentence per line is ideal on LinkedIn.
- White space is your friend. Dense blocks of text get scrolled past immediately.
- Write like you are telling a story to a trusted colleague, not writing a corporate memo
- Include a turning point or "aha moment" in the narrative that creates emotional resonance
- Specificity builds credibility: "We tested 14 approaches over 3 months" beats "We tried many things"
- End with a question that invites genuine professional discussion, not surface-level engagement bait

### What Performs Best on LinkedIn
- Personal stories with professional lessons extracted from real experience
- Contrarian takes on accepted industry norms backed by reasoning or data
- "Here's what I learned when..." narratives with specific, measurable outcomes
- Frameworks and mental models that people save for future reference (saves are the strongest signal in 2026)
- Behind-the-scenes of real business decisions, especially ones that went wrong
- Hot takes that challenge conventional wisdom with clear reasoning

### Key Stats
- Saves have become the most important engagement signal on LinkedIn in 2026
- Personal profiles generate 8x more engagement than company pages for the same content
- Posts with external links receive 40% less initial reach; the algorithm wants users to stay on-platform
- LinkedIn posts have a 24-hour half-life (compared to X's 18 minutes), meaning content works longer
- 80% of posts should be native content with no links; when links are needed, place them in the first comment
