# X TEXT POST: Platform-Native Content Knowledge
## Platform: X (Twitter) | Format: Text Post | Last Updated: April 2026

---

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Tweet Copy**: The tweet text. Maximum 280 characters for standard users (up to 25,000 for X Premium). Every character counts. No filler words. Direct and opinionated.

2. **Engagement Hook**: Why someone would reply or retweet. Built into the tweet itself: a question, a hot take, an incomplete thought, or a direct request for input.

3. **Hashtags**: 1-2 maximum. Fewer is better on X. Hashtags can be mid-sentence or at end. Never more than 2.

4. **Quote Tweet Bait**: What makes this tweet worth adding your own take to. Strong opinions that people want to agree with, disagree with, or build on.

---

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES

### What Works on X in 2026
- X's algorithm favors niche topical authority and real-time participation in conversations
- Paid verification (X Premium) provides a visibility boost; content from verified accounts gets more distribution
- The platform dynamically adapts feeds to users' shifting interests throughout the day
- Niche-focused content consistently outperforms broad messaging
- X deprioritizes tweets with external links; the algorithm favors content that keeps users on-platform

### Copy Rules
- Brevity is the soul of X. If you can say it in 140 characters, do not use 280.
- Strong opinions perform well: "Hot take: [contrarian view]" is a format that consistently drives engagement
- Questions drive replies: "What's the most underrated [topic in your industry]?" is simple but effective
- Lists and observations: "Things nobody tells you about [topic]:" followed by a thread indicator
- Use line breaks for readability even within 280 characters
- Avoid links in the main tweet body when possible; X deprioritizes tweets with external links. Put links in the first reply instead.
- Contractions, informal grammar, and conversational tone perform better than formal writing
- Likes are now private by default on X; engagement is measured through replies, reposts, quote posts, and bookmarks

### Engagement Optimization
- Post timing matters more on X than any other platform due to its real-time nature
- Reply to comments within the first 30 minutes for maximum algorithmic boost
- Short, punchy tweets that invite a reply outperform long, complete-thought tweets
- The best tweets feel like one side of a conversation, inviting the other person to complete it
