## CONTENT QUALITY RULES

1. **Specificity Over Generality**: "Grew revenue 43% in Q3" beats "significantly improved results." "Tested 14 fabric blends over 8 months" beats "carefully developed our product." Numbers, names, and concrete details build credibility.

2. **Human Over Corporate**: Write like a person talking to another person. Not like a brand guidelines document, not like a press release, not like a committee approved the copy.

3. **Platform-Native Formatting**: Every platform has different text formatting norms. LinkedIn uses aggressive line breaks (one sentence per line). X is concise and punchy. Instagram captions use emojis and line breaks for readability. Never apply one platform's formatting to another.

4. **CTA Variation**: Never use the same call-to-action in two consecutive posts. Rotate between: save, share, comment, follow, DM, link, tag someone. Each CTA should match the post's intent.

5. **Hashtag Restraint**: More platforms are de-emphasizing hashtags in favor of keyword-rich captions for content discovery. Use hashtags sparingly and strategically, never as a growth lever.

6. **Caption Keywords**: Instagram, TikTok, and LinkedIn now use AI to understand caption content for search ranking. Include relevant keywords naturally in captions rather than relying on hashtags.

7. **No Generic Content**: If a post could belong to any brand in the same category, it is too generic. Remove the brand name; if the content still makes sense for a competitor, start over with something more specific.

8. **No Repeated Concepts**: Within a single month's calendar, every post must have a distinct concept, hook, and creative direction. No two posts should feel like variations of the same idea.

9. **Every Post Must Earn Its Place**: Before generating any content, the AI should verify the post has a clear strategic purpose within the calendar. If the answer is "to fill a slot," reject the idea and find a real one.

## ALGORITHM UNIVERSAL TRUTHS (2026)

These patterns hold true across all major social media platforms:

1. **Engagement quality over volume**: 200 saves beat 2,000 likes. 15 thoughtful comments beat 300 fire emojis. Every platform now measures the DEPTH of engagement, not just the count.

2. **DM shares as the new virality**: Content that people send privately to friends is the most powerful distribution mechanism across Instagram, X, and LinkedIn. Design content that someone would want to send to a specific person.

3. **Native content wins**: Every platform deprioritizes content with external links. Native video beats YouTube links. Native images beat link previews. Keep users on-platform.

4. **Authenticity premium**: As AI-generated content floods every platform, content that feels human, raw, and specific stands out more than ever. The "overproduced corporate" look is losing to the "real person sharing real experience" look.

5. **Saves signal lasting value**: Across Instagram, LinkedIn, and Pinterest, saves are the strongest quality signal for algorithms. Content that gets saved is treated as high-value reference material and receives extended distribution.

6. **Completion rate is king for video**: On every video-supporting platform (Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, TikTok, LinkedIn Video), completion rate (percentage of viewers who watch to the end) is the primary algorithmic ranking signal. A short video watched completely always outperforms a long video abandoned early.

7. **First impression window is shrinking**: Instagram gives you 3 seconds. LinkedIn gives you 2 lines before "see more." X gives you the first sentence in a crowded feed. The hook is no longer important. The hook IS the content.
