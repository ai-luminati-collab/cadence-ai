# CROSS-PLATFORM POSTING INTELLIGENCE
## Logic Framework for Multi-Platform Content Distribution | Last Updated: April 2026

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Cross-posting is NOT about copying content across platforms. It is about recognizing that a single content CONCEPT can have multiple platform-native EXECUTIONS. The concept travels. The execution adapts.

Example: The concept "We just hit 10,000 customers" is platform-agnostic. But the execution is radically different:
- Instagram: Celebration Reel with team footage, confetti, customer montage. Caption focuses on gratitude and community.
- LinkedIn: Founder narrative post about the journey, lessons learned, what almost killed the company at 1,000 customers. Professional reflection.
- X: Punchy tweet. "10,000 customers. 0 paid ads. Here's the one thing that worked →" with a tactical breakdown thread.
- Stories: Countdown leading up to announcement, poll asking "What should we do to celebrate?"

Same milestone. Four completely different pieces of content. This is the correct approach.

## CONTENT INTENT CLASSIFICATION

Every post has a primary INTENT. The intent determines which platforms it belongs on and how it adapts.

### INTENT 1: THOUGHT LEADERSHIP / OPINION
"Here's what I think about [industry topic]"

- LinkedIn: PRIMARY. Algorithm rewards professional insight, contrarian takes. Saves and comments drive distribution. Format: long-form narrative with personal experience and data.
- X: STRONG. Rewards real-time opinions and niche authority. Format: tweet or thread. Punchy, direct, slightly provocative.
- Instagram: CONDITIONAL. Only works if the brand has a strong personal or founder voice and the audience expects depth. Format: carousel with key points visualized.
- Facebook: WEAK. Skip unless the brand has an active community group where discussions thrive.

AI Rule: If intent is THOUGHT LEADERSHIP, generate for LinkedIn first. Offer X thread as secondary. Only suggest Instagram if the brand profile includes "founder-led" or "personal brand" as a content pillar.

### INTENT 2: EDUCATIONAL / HOW-TO / TIPS
"Here's how to do [useful thing]"

- Instagram: PRIMARY. Carousels are the #1 save-driver. Educational content gets saved and shared. Reels work for quick single-tip demos.
- LinkedIn: STRONG. Educational posts get saved heavily. Frameworks and processes drive engagement. Format: document post (PDF carousel) or text post for single insights.
- X: MODERATE. Works as a thread for step-by-step breakdowns. Less effective as single tweet unless very concise.
- Pinterest: STRONG. Evergreen educational content performs exceptionally well. Format: infographic-style pin with keyword-rich description.
- YouTube Shorts: MODERATE. Only if the content involves a visual demonstration that benefits from video.

AI Rule: If intent is EDUCATIONAL, generate for Instagram (carousel) first. Suggest LinkedIn (document post) as secondary. Offer X thread and Pinterest pin as tertiary.

### INTENT 3: PRODUCT SHOWCASE / LAUNCH
"Look at what we made / Here's our new thing"

- Instagram: PRIMARY. Visual-first platform. Reel for demo/unboxing, static for hero shot, carousel for features breakdown, Stories for countdown and launch hype.
- Pinterest: STRONG for e-commerce brands. Product pins with shopping integration drive direct purchase.
- Facebook: MODERATE. Works for brands with existing following. Good for driving purchase with link posts.
- LinkedIn: CONDITIONAL. Only if the product is B2B relevant or the launch has a business story behind it. Frame as a business story: "We spent 8 months rebuilding our formula. Here's why."
- X: MODERATE. Announcement tweet with compelling image. Thread for backstory if there's one worth telling.

AI Rule: If intent is PRODUCT, generate for Instagram first. Suggest Pinterest for e-commerce. Only suggest LinkedIn if there's a B2B angle or a founder/business narrative. Never post raw product promotion on LinkedIn without a story wrapper.

### INTENT 4: BEHIND-THE-SCENES / CULTURE
"Here's what it's like inside our brand"

- Instagram: PRIMARY. Stories and Reels are perfect for BTS content. Informal, authentic, human.
- LinkedIn: STRONG. "How we work" content humanizes a brand. Frame around a professional insight: "Here's why we do X differently."
- X: WEAK. Only if there's a specific interesting, funny, or surprising moment worth a standalone tweet.
- Facebook: MODERATE. Works in community groups and for building brand affinity.

AI Rule: If intent is BTS/CULTURE, generate for Instagram (Stories or Reel) first. Suggest LinkedIn only if the BTS moment contains a professional insight or lesson. Skip X unless the moment is tweet-worthy standalone.

### INTENT 5: TOPICAL / MOMENT MARKETING
"Something is happening in culture/news and we're commenting on it"

- X: PRIMARY. Real-time conversation platform. Speed matters enormously. First-mover advantage is critical.
- Instagram: STRONG if the brand can create a quick visual (meme, graphic, Reel) tying the moment to the brand. Must be fast; topicals lose relevance within 24-48 hours.
- Threads: STRONG. Real-time conversation platform good for quick takes and discussion.
- LinkedIn: CONDITIONAL. Only if the topical is professionally relevant (industry event, economic news, policy change). Professional commentary format.

AI Rule: If intent is TOPICAL, generate for X first (speed is everything). Suggest Instagram if the brand can create a quick visual. Only suggest LinkedIn if the topic is professionally relevant. Never force a topical onto a platform where the audience doesn't expect it.

### INTENT 6: USER STORIES / TESTIMONIALS / SOCIAL PROOF
"Our customers love us, here's proof"

- Instagram: PRIMARY. UGC Reels, customer quote carousels, before/after transformations.
- Facebook: STRONG. Social proof drives community engagement and purchase consideration.
- LinkedIn: CONDITIONAL. Works for B2B case studies or when framed as "what we learned from helping [customer]." Data-driven results, professional framing.
- X: MODERATE. Quick proof tweets: "Customer just told us [quote]. This is why we do what we do." with screenshot.

AI Rule: If intent is SOCIAL PROOF, generate for Instagram first. Suggest Facebook for community engagement. Only suggest LinkedIn for B2B case study framing.

### INTENT 7: ENTERTAINMENT / RELATABLE / MEME
"Something funny, relatable, or culturally resonant"

- Instagram: PRIMARY. Reels and meme-style statics drive DM shares, which is the strongest algorithmic signal.
- X: STRONG. Witty, shareable tweets thrive. Meme format with brand twist.
- Threads: STRONG. Casual, conversational platform perfect for relatable content.
- LinkedIn: ALMOST NEVER. Unless it's "professional humor" that's genuinely clever and industry-specific. Most memes feel off-brand on LinkedIn and damage credibility.
- Facebook: MODERATE. Works in community groups.

AI Rule: If intent is ENTERTAINMENT, generate for Instagram (Reel or static) first. Suggest X as secondary. Only suggest LinkedIn if humor is specifically professional and the brand has an established playful LinkedIn voice.

### INTENT 8: COMMUNITY / CONVERSATION STARTER
"We want to hear from our audience"

- Instagram: PRIMARY. Stories with interactive stickers (polls, questions, quizzes) are built for this. Feed posts with questions drive comments.
- LinkedIn: STRONG. Discussion-oriented posts perform well. Must use specific, thought-provoking questions.
- X: STRONG. Quick polls, open questions, "This or that" format.
- Facebook: STRONG. Groups are built for community engagement.

AI Rule: If intent is COMMUNITY, generate for whichever platform has the brand's most active community first. Default to Instagram Stories for quick engagement, LinkedIn for professional discussions, X for quick polls.

## CROSS-POST TYPE CLASSIFICATION

Every post gets one of three cross-post classifications:

**concept-adapt**: The concept works across platforms but needs a different execution for each. Generate separate platform-native drafts with different copy, format, tone, and CTA. Example: an educational tip that becomes an Instagram carousel, a LinkedIn document post, and an X thread.

**native-only**: This content is specific to one platform's format or culture. Do not cross-post. Examples: Instagram Stories (ephemeral, interactive), X real-time trending commentary (time-sensitive, platform-specific tone), LinkedIn personal narratives that would feel out of place on Instagram.

**universal**: Rare. Only for major announcements where the message is genuinely platform-agnostic (brand name change, major funding, critical product launch). Even then, adapt the format, length, tone, and CTA for each platform.

## AI IMPLEMENTATION RULES

1. NEVER generate identical content for two platforms. If a post is tagged for cross-platform distribution, generate a separate draft for each platform with different copy, format, and CTA.

2. ALWAYS identify the primary platform first. The content intent determines which platform is primary. The concept is born there. Other platforms receive adapted versions.

3. SHOW the user which posts can travel. In the calendar view, posts with cross-platform potential should display a badge showing which other platforms they could be adapted for. User clicks to generate the adapted version.

4. RESPECT platform culture. A casual, emoji-heavy Instagram caption must become a professional, narrative-driven LinkedIn post. Same concept. Different language. Different energy. Different structure.

5. TRACK platform-specific fields. Each platform adaptation generates its own set of platformFields. An Instagram Reel has hookScript, bodyScript, musicDirection. An X adaptation has tweetCopy and hashtags. A LinkedIn version has hook, bodyNarrative, closingCTA.

6. FLAG exclusions explicitly and explain why. If a post should NOT go on a platform, tell the user the reason. Example: "This personal founder story won't resonate on X where the audience expects quick, opinionated takes rather than long narratives." This builds trust in the AI's strategic judgment.
