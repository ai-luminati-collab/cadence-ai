# META REEL: Platform-Native Content Knowledge
## Platform: Instagram/Facebook | Format: Reel | Last Updated: April 2026

---

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

When generating content for a Meta Reel, the AI must produce these specific fields:

1. **Hook Script (0-3 seconds)**: The spoken/shown content for the first 3 seconds. Must create a pattern interrupt or curiosity gap. Maximum 15 words spoken. Include visual direction for what the viewer sees.

2. **Body Script (3-20 seconds)**: Main content delivery. Delivers on the hook's promise. One core idea only. Provide scene-by-scene breakdown with timing for each scene.

3. **CTA Script (final 3-5 seconds)**: Closing call to action. Must be specific and single-action. Not always "follow for more." Vary based on the post's purpose (save, share, comment, visit link, DM).

4. **On-Screen Text Cues**: Text overlays synchronized with spoken word. Maximum 6 words per text card. Used for emphasis and key points, not as full subtitles. Include timestamps.

5. **Music Direction**: Specify trending audio vs original voiceover. Include mood descriptor (energetic, calm, dramatic, playful). BPM range if relevant. Note any specific audio trends in the brand's niche.

6. **Caption**: Post caption below the Reel. 50-150 words. Structure: hook line, value expansion, CTA. 3-5 hashtags at the end after a line break.

7. **Hashtags**: 3-5 total. Mix: 1 high-volume (500K+), 2 mid-volume (50K-500K), 1-2 niche (under 50K). Caption keywords matter more than hashtags in 2026.

8. **Visual Directive**: Shot-by-shot visual direction. Camera angles, transitions between scenes, B-roll notes, color grading/mood guidance, product placement if relevant.

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES

### Length and Pacing
- Optimal length for maximum completion rate and algorithmic distribution: 15-30 seconds
- Shorter Reels (7-15 seconds) work for punchy hooks and simple messages but may lack sufficient watch time signal for the algorithm
- Longer Reels (30-60 seconds) require exceptionally strong hooks to maintain retention; only use for in-depth educational content
- A 15-second Reel watched to completion will always outperform a 60-second Reel with 30% retention
- Cut or transition every 2-3 seconds to maintain visual interest and prevent drop-off
- Instagram allows Reels up to 3 minutes, but longer is rarely better

### The 3-Second Hook
- Up to 50% of viewers drop off in the first 3 seconds
- Reels with 3-second hold rates above 60% outperform those below 40% by 5-10x in total reach
- The viewer's brain is in a low-attention autopilot state while scrolling; hooks must create "pattern interrupts" that force the brain from passive to active attention
- Effective hook types: bold claim ("This one strategy 3x'd our revenue"), pattern interrupt (unexpected visual or camera angle), curiosity gap (partial reveal that demands completion), contrarian statement ("Stop doing X right now")
- Hooks that fail every time: slow intros, logo animations, "Hey guys" or generic greetings, title cards, throat-clearing preambles, asking "Did you know..."
- Combining visual AND verbal interrupts creates the strongest effect (close-up + counterintuitive statement)
- Research shows digital content has roughly 1.7 seconds to capture interest before users decide to move on

### Audio Strategy
- Ideal mix: 60% trending audio / 40% original audio
- Trending audio provides an algorithmic distribution boost and signals cultural relevance; use for trend participation and product showcases
- Original audio (voiceover, brand sound) builds unique brand identity and creates reusable audio assets
- 85% of Reels are watched without sound initially, so text supers are mandatory for comprehension
- Clear speech with keyword-rich voiceovers helps Instagram categorize and rank content in search (Instagram SEO)

### Algorithm Signals (Ranked by Importance for 2026)
1. Completion rate / watch time (the strongest signal by far)
2. Shares via DMs (the strongest distribution signal for discovery and reaching non-followers)
3. Saves (indicator of long-term value; drives extended reach)
4. Comments (conversation quality matters more than comment count)
5. Likes (the weakest signal; almost irrelevant for algorithmic distribution)

### Visual and Technical Rules
- 9:16 vertical format is mandatory; any content with horizontal bars or square format kills performance
- No watermarks from other platforms (TikTok, CapCut logos); Instagram penalizes watermarked content by 40-60% in reach
- Instagram's "Originality Score" in 2026 detects recycled clips; reposting TikTok videos verbatim will significantly tank reach
- Always add a unique spin or create original content
- Feature people in Reels whenever possible; human faces and UGC-style content performs significantly better
- Reels generate 36% more reach than carousels and significantly more than static posts

### Best Practices Summary
- Target 70%+ watch-through rate for viral potential; 45-60% is solid; below 30% means the hook or pacing needs a complete restructure
- Track retention graphs to identify exact drop-off points
- Reels are designed for DISCOVERY (reaching non-followers); Stories are for RETENTION (engaging existing followers)
- Posts without hashtags achieved 23% higher reach than hashtag-heavy posts in recent studies; use hashtags for categorization only, not as a growth lever
- Instagram now uses AI to understand caption content for search ranking; include relevant keywords naturally in captions
