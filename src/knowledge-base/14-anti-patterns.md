# 14 — Anti-Pattern Database

> Things the AI must NEVER do. This file grows over time as patterns are detected.
> Each anti-pattern includes WHY it fails and WHAT to do instead.

---

## HOOK ANTI-PATTERNS

### ANTI-PATTERN: Starting with "Did you know...?"
**Why it fails**: "Did you know" is the most overused hook format on social media. It signals lazy content and users have been conditioned to scroll past it. It also assumes ignorance, which can feel condescending.
**Instead do**: State the surprising fact as a declaration. "Most people add turmeric at the wrong stage" is 5x more engaging than "Did you know you might be adding turmeric at the wrong stage?"

### ANTI-PATTERN: Starting a LinkedIn post with "I'm excited to announce"
**Why it fails**: This opening signals corporate press release energy. LinkedIn's algorithm deprioritizes content that reads like PR. Users scroll past it reflexively. Data shows these posts get 40-60% less engagement.
**Instead do**: Lead with the outcome, the lesson, or the most surprising part. "We almost didn't build this product. Then one customer email changed everything." gets 5x the engagement.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Opening with a rhetorical question on Reels
**Why it fails**: "Are you tired of boring spices?" — nobody answers rhetorical questions. They scroll. Rhetorical questions waste the critical first 0.5 seconds where the hook needs to land.
**Instead do**: Make a bold declaration that invites disagreement. "Your spice rack is lying to you" stops the scroll.

### ANTI-PATTERN: "Here are X tips/hacks/ways to..."
**Why it fails**: Listicle hooks are the most saturated format on every platform. They communicate "generic content ahead." The viewer has seen this format 10,000 times.
**Instead do**: Name the enemy. "The one ingredient ruining your biryani (and you add it every time)" is specific, creates loss aversion, and implies insider knowledge.

## CAPTION ANTI-PATTERNS

### ANTI-PATTERN: Using AI smog words
**Banned words**: "Elevate," "Unlock," "Supercharge," "Transform," "Revolutionary," "Game-changer," "Dive in," "Journey," "Delight," "Experience," "Seamless," "Empower," "Cutting-edge," "Best-in-class."
**Why they fail**: These words are a dead giveaway for AI-generated content. They have zero specificity and create no mental image in the reader's mind. They are the linguistic equivalent of stock photos.
**Instead do**: Use concrete, sensory language. Instead of "elevate your cooking," say "that first bite where everyone at the table goes quiet." Paint a picture.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Generic value propositions
**Example**: "We provide high-quality products at affordable prices."
**Why it fails**: Every brand in every industry claims this. It communicates nothing unique. The reader cannot distinguish this brand from any competitor.
**Instead do**: Name the specific differentiator. "38 years of sourcing from the same 4 farms in Maharashtra" is a brand truth. "High quality" is not.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Multiple exclamation marks
**Example**: "Check out our amazing new collection!! You won't regret it!!!"
**Why it fails**: Studies show excessive exclamation marks reduce perceived credibility by 30%. It reads as desperate and performative. One is fine. Three is a red flag.
**Instead do**: Let the content carry the enthusiasm. Strong writing doesn't need punctuation to yell.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Same CTA on consecutive posts
**Why it fails**: If every post ends with "Shop now at [link]," the audience learns to ignore the last line. CTA fatigue is real — engagement on CTAs drops 60% when they're repetitive.
**Instead do**: Vary CTAs. Use "Save this," "Tag someone who," "Drop a 🌶️ if," "Tell me in comments," "Link in bio for the recipe." Alternate between engagement-CTAs and conversion-CTAs.

## FORMAT ANTI-PATTERNS

### ANTI-PATTERN: Using a carousel when a Reel would work better
**When carousels fail**: When the content is a single insight or story that doesn't benefit from pagination. Forcing a simple idea into 8 slides dilutes the impact.
**Rule of thumb**: If the content is better spoken or shown in motion → Reel. If the content benefits from sequential reading or reference-saving → Carousel.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Making a 60-second Reel when 15 seconds would suffice
**Why it fails**: Platform data shows completion rate drops 50% after 15 seconds for content that doesn't have a strong narrative arc. Padding a simple idea to fill time is worse than cutting it short.
**Instead do**: Match length to content density. One recipe hack = 15s. Full recipe walkthrough = 45-60s. Story with setup-tension-payoff = 30-45s.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Static image posts on Instagram in 2025-2026
**Why it fails**: Instagram's algorithm has deprioritized static images to less than 15% of reach compared to Reels. A static image is functionally invisible unless it's a data visualization or infographic worth saving.
**Exception**: Minimalist typography quotes and data-heavy carousels still perform because they are save-worthy.

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC ANTI-PATTERNS

### ANTI-PATTERN: Cross-posting the same content to all platforms
**Why it fails**: Each platform has different audience behavior, content format, and algorithmic preferences. A Reel caption doesn't work as a LinkedIn post. A tweet doesn't work as a Pinterest pin. Cross-posting signals laziness and each platform's algorithm penalizes non-native content.
**Instead do**: Create platform-native versions of the same IDEA, not copies of the same CONTENT.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Using hashtags on LinkedIn
**Why it fails**: LinkedIn's current algorithm gives negligible reach benefit to hashtags. Using more than 3 actually signals "bot content" and can reduce visibility. The platform has shifted to pure feed-algorithm distribution.
**Instead do**: Use 0-3 highly specific hashtags maximum. Focus on hook quality and comment engagement instead.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Thread-baiting on X without delivering
**Example**: "I spent 10 years studying the spice industry. Here's everything I learned 🧵" followed by obvious, surface-level observations.
**Why it fails**: Thread-baiting (big promise, shallow delivery) destroys trust. X's algorithm now factors in engagement dropoff between tweet 1 and tweet 3+. If people stop reading, future tweets get suppressed.
**Instead do**: Front-load the best insight in tweet 1-2. If the first two tweets aren't individually strong enough to go viral, the thread is too weak.

### ANTI-PATTERN: YouTube Shorts with no retention hook in first frame
**Why it fails**: YouTube Shorts algorithm is 90% driven by average-view-duration. If the first frame doesn't create an "I need to see what happens" feeling, the viewer swipes instantly and the algorithm kills the short.
**Instead do**: Open with motion, conflict, or a visual pattern interrupt. Never open with text overlays or a face talking to camera without visual context.

## BRAND VOICE ANTI-PATTERNS

### ANTI-PATTERN: Switching voice between platforms without intention
**Why it fails**: Brand voice inconsistency confuses the audience and weakens brand recall. If Instagram is playful but LinkedIn is corporate, the brand has no recognizable voice.
**Exception**: Tone INTENSITY can vary (LinkedIn = more professional, Instagram = more casual), but the core voice characteristics (humor style, vocabulary, perspective) should be consistent.

### ANTI-PATTERN: Using formal language for a brand with a casual persona
**Example**: A street food brand posting "We are pleased to inform our valued customers that we have introduced new variants."
**Why it fails**: The language doesn't match the brand energy. It creates cognitive dissonance and feels fake.
**Instead do**: "New flavours just dropped. Your taste buds aren't ready. 🔥"

### ANTI-PATTERN: Being "relatable" without being specific
**Example**: "We get it. Life is hard. That's why our product exists."
**Why it fails**: Fake empathy without specificity. Every brand "gets it." None of them show HOW they get it. This reads as patronizing rather than connecting.
**Instead do**: Name the SPECIFIC moment. "That 6 PM moment when you open the fridge, stare for 30 seconds, and close it again — that's when Spicezzaa saves dinner."
