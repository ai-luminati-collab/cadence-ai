# LINKEDIN VIDEO: Platform-Native Content Knowledge
## Platform: LinkedIn | Format: Native Video | Last Updated: April 2026

---

## DRAFT FIELDS FOR AI GENERATION

1. **Hook Script (0-3 seconds)**: What stops the scroll. Must lead with a problem, insight, or bold statement. Never start with "Hey everyone," a logo animation, or a generic greeting. Drop the viewer directly into the most compelling moment.

2. **Body Script**: Main video content. One core idea delivered clearly. Scene-by-scene breakdown with timing. Optimal length: 30-90 seconds for discovery content, 2-5 minutes for audiences who already follow the brand.

3. **Closing CTA**: Final moment of the video. Specific action: comment prompt, follow CTA, "DM me for [specific resource]," or "Save this for when you need it."

4. **Caption Overlays**: On-screen text and supers. Mandatory because 80% of LinkedIn users watch video on mute. Must be burned-in captions (not relying on platform auto-captions). Maximum 6 words per overlay, synchronized with key moments in the script.

5. **Post Caption**: Written caption below the video. 100-200 words. Hook in the first 2 lines (before the "see more" truncation). Expand on the video's theme with additional context. End with a discussion question that invites comments.

6. **Hashtags**: 3-5 industry-specific hashtags in the caption.

7. **Thumbnail Direction**: Custom thumbnail guidance. Use a high-contrast image with readable text overlay and ideally a human face. Never use auto-generated thumbnail; a blurry mid-sentence frame kills click-through.

## RESEARCH-BACKED RULES

### LinkedIn Video Algorithm (2026)
- Native video receives 5x more engagement than static posts on average
- LinkedIn prioritizes: native uploads (never YouTube/Vimeo links), dwell time (how long someone watches), and educational content over promotional content
- Posts with external links (including YouTube links) receive 30-40% less initial reach because the algorithm deprioritizes content that pulls users off-platform
- The "Golden Hour": LinkedIn tests your content with a small slice of your network in the first 60 minutes. Strong early engagement (especially thoughtful comments) determines whether it expands to broader distribution. Weak early engagement kills the post.
- Responding to comments within 15 minutes of posting triggers a significant algorithmic boost by signaling active, genuine engagement
- LinkedIn's algorithm analyzes engagement patterns to detect artificial engagement pods; genuine conversations receive priority distribution

### Format and Technical Specs
- Vertical format (9:16, 1080x1920px) is currently receiving a distribution boost over horizontal on LinkedIn
- 4:5 ratio (1080x1350px) is the optimal standard for mobile feed: takes up nearly the entire vertical screen without triggering full-screen immersive mode, meaning post copy and the comment section remain visible (this is strategically important for driving comments)
- MP4 format with H.264 video codec and AAC audio is mandatory
- Maximum file size: 5GB (recommended under 1GB for faster uploads and processing)
- Length limits: minimum 3 seconds, maximum 10 minutes for feed posts
- Sweet spots: 30-60 seconds for quick tips and discovery content, 2-5 minutes for in-depth content targeting existing followers
- Frame rate: 30fps recommended (up to 60fps supported)

### Content Types That Perform Well
- Quick tips (30-60 seconds): single actionable tactic explained clearly and directly
- Behind-the-scenes: showing your process, tools, workspace, or team in action
- Expert perspectives: opinion on industry trends, hot takes on common practices, "Here's what everyone gets wrong about..."
- Case study walkthroughs: screen recordings showing real results with narration
- "Here's what happened when..." narrative format with a clear before/after arc
- Personal stories with professional lessons: vulnerability combined with expertise
- Founder stories: journey, struggles, lessons that humanize the brand

### What to Avoid
- Overly produced, corporate-style video. Raw authenticity consistently beats polish on LinkedIn in 2026.
- Starting with a logo animation or company intro. Lead with the insight or problem immediately.
- Promotional product pitches without an educational or story-driven wrapper. LinkedIn actively penalizes promotional content.
- Videos without captions. 80% of LinkedIn users watch on mute. This is non-negotiable.
- Horizontal/landscape format for new content. Vertical gets the distribution boost.
- External video links (YouTube, Vimeo). Always upload natively.

### Critical Context
- Video views on LinkedIn dropped 36% year-over-year across business pages (SocialInsider 2026 benchmark report based on 1.3 million posts). BUT this is because most brands are still posting horizontal, uncaptioned, promotional video that the algorithm deprioritizes.
- The opportunity: the bar is low. Brands that execute LinkedIn video correctly (native, vertical, captioned, educational, authentic) immediately stand out.
- 78% of B2B buyers prefer messages delivered via video over text (only 9% prefer text)
- Native documents (PDF carousels) are technically the #1 format for engagement rate at 7%, but video dominates for building trust, personal brand authority, and driving profile visits
